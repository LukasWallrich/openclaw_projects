One Italian-side answer near Lugano

The closest Italian analogue to the Ticino balcony routes is not a single ready-made four-day trail. It is a carefully shortened Val d’Intelvi traverse, borrowing the marked Via dei Monti Lariani and the valley’s village paths, with Lake Lugano as the natural finish.

Verdict

Best candidate: Val d’Intelvi → Porlezza

This is the only close Italian option I found that can plausibly preserve the brief after splitting the long official stages. It gives chestnut woods, stone villages, old military roads, open balconies and views of both Ceresio (Lake Lugano) and Lake Como, while keeping the group in inhabited valleys rather than remote huts.

Important honesty: the canonical Via dei Monti Lariani is not a clean fit. The official Lombardy listing gives 6½–8½-hour stages, and the route follows the western shore of Lake Como. The plan below is therefore a custom, adjusted option — not a claim that the published route itself satisfies every requirement.

Option I · Italian near-miss, made workable

Intelvi balcony traverse — Argegno / Pigra → Porlezza

Use a short ferry/bus approach to avoid the overlong Cernobbio start, then walk west and north through the Italian valley. The final day can drop to Porlezza, the small Italian town on Lake Lugano’s northern arm; from there Lugano is a straightforward bus connection. The official San Fedele–Lenno page publishes these figures and supplies a GPX. I downloaded the GPX: it contains track geometry but no elevation points, so I have not substituted a noisy digital-elevation estimate. The full stage therefore fails the stated ascent and descent caps; splitting at Boffalora is necessary, but the two custom halves still need a current GPX/SwissTopo-style profile before booking.

What is now verified: the official route reaches Boffalora at 9.7 km / 1,216 m after the published intermediate points La Zerla 1,144 m, Bassetta Bassa 1,300 m and Alpe Colonno 1,334 m. What remains provisional: days 1, 2 and the Boffalora → Porlezza connection, including exact gain/loss, surface and 2026 transport.

Sleep and logistics

  • Access: Lugano → Menaggio/Argegno area by public transport; the Argegno–Pigra cable car is the useful approach seam. Timetables should be checked for the 2026 summer season.
  • Beds: target hotels/B&Bs in Schignano, San Fedele, and Lanzo/Pellio or Claino. This is materially more private-room friendly than the hut-based Ticino alternatives.
  • Finish: Porlezza has regular onward bus links toward Lugano; treat the final connection as a transport day, not as part of the hiking mileage.
  • Heat: low Italian valleys and open south-facing slopes will be hot in July/August. Start early and preserve the cable-car/bus escape options.

Comparison

Why I did not promote the full Via dei Monti Lariani

The official itinerary is attractive and close — Cernobbio to Sorico, in seven published sections — but it fails the brief in three places: the first listed section is 7½ hours, several later sections are 8½ hours, and the route’s defining lake is Como. More importantly, the official San Fedele–Lenno section is 18.9 km with +1,226 m / −1,763 m, so it fails both elevation caps. The Italian option is therefore a useful lead and a possible custom split, not a verified replacement for H1/H2.

Result: worth keeping as an extra option, but not as a replacement for H1/H2. H1 Strada Alta Leventina remains the cleaner low-stress choice; this Italian option wins on Italian villages, private beds and proximity to Lugano, but loses on route certainty and the explicit Lake Como exclusion.
