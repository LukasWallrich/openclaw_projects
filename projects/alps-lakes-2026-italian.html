The Italian-side answer near Lugano

A real, marked route does exist: the Sentiero delle 4 Valli crosses Val Sanagra, Val Cavargna, Val Rezzo and Valsolda from Breglia to Dasio above Lake Lugano. Its published three stages become a much better four-day plan when the overlong middle stage is split at San Nazzaro.

Verdict

Best candidate: Sentiero delle 4 Valli

This is materially stronger than the Val d’Intelvi proposal: the route is documented by local tourism and regional forest sources, has a coherent point-to-point line, reaches the Italian side of Lake Lugano, and has a natural split that turns its only 8-hour stage into two manageable days.

Verdict: this is the Italian option I would actually investigate for booking. It meets the walking-time brief after the split, stays below the stated elevation ceiling in the published route data, and avoids a Lake Como lake-stay. Option I · strongest Italian-side candidate

Sentiero delle 4 Valli — Breglia → Dasio

4 walking days4:20–6:00 h/day published/split targetE / T2 target no high-alpine ridge requiredvillage stays plus possible apartment

Start at Breglia above Menaggio, walk through Val Sanagra and Val Cavargna, then cross Val Rezzo into Valsolda. Finish at Dasio, above the Italian shore of Lake Lugano; continue by bus to Porlezza/Lugano. The route’s high point is the Monti Colonè at 1,400 m, not an exposed summit.

The main route page publishes day 1 as 15 km / 5:30 / 782 m global elevation, day 2 as 19 km / 8:00 / 460 m, and day 4 (its original third stage) as 13 km / 5:00 / 517 m. A second local source gives day 1 as 13 km / 6:00 and day 4 as 7:00, so the published data are not perfectly consistent. The 8-hour middle stage is split at San Nazzaro using its named timing points: Malè→Tecchio 3:40, Tecchio→San Nazzaro about 40 minutes, and Tecchio→Cavargna about 4:20. The route’s official GPX download contains elevation waypoints but is sparse; use it with a current map profile before booking rather than treating it as survey-grade. No published figure exceeds the 1,000 m ascent ceiling, and the route’s 1,400 m high point makes a >1,500 m descent day unlikely, but the split-day ascent/descent pair is not published.

Accommodation check: Malè has Locanda Maria; Cavargna has local lodging and the bus stop is 100 m from the route end. San Nazzaro has listed private apartments/holiday homes, but the municipality’s own hospitality page is currently marked “in update”, so this is the one call-first night. The fallback is to sleep in San Bartolomeo/San Nazzaro and use the C14 for the short access seam.

Sleep and logistics

  • Access: Menaggio → Breglia by C13; the route page identifies Breglia as the bus start. From Lugano, reach Menaggio/Porlezza by public transport and reverse the connection.
  • Beds: Locanda Maria at Malè; private apartment/B&B options listed for San Nazzaro; local B&B/guesthouse options in Cavargna. Call before committing to dates.
  • Finish: Dasio is above Lake Lugano; use the local C12/Ceresio network to reach Porlezza and Lugano. Verify the summer 2026 timetable.
  • Technical line: stay on the marked 4 Valli route. Do not add the Alta Via del Lario or Pizzo di Gino variants: those are rated EE and include exposed/rocky or fixed-rope terrain.

Comparison

Why this beats the earlier Val d’Intelvi idea

The Via dei Monti Lariani remains attractive but its official sections are long, and its defining scenery is Lake Como. The Sentiero delle 4 Valli is the better match because it was specifically built to connect the western mountain paths to the Swiss-border side, ends above Lake Lugano, and has a published 8-hour stage that can be split at a real road/bus access point rather than guessed at a hut.

Result: a genuine extra option, but not yet a “book without checking” option. H1 Strada Alta Leventina is still the cleaner low-stress choice. This Italian route now deserves a serious comparison because the underlying route is real; the remaining work is concrete: profile the downloaded GPX at the San Nazzaro split, confirm the two timing discrepancies, and secure the San Nazzaro night.
